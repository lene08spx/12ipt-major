Introduction

Specifying Velocity

We can think of velocity in terms of a speed and an angle of inclination.
\(s\tan\theta\)

But we can talk about velocity in terms of horizontal and vertical components:
\(\utilde{v}=\dot{x}\utilde{i}+\dot{y}\utilde{j}\) where \(\dot{x}=\utilde{v}\cdotp\utidle{i}\) and \(\dot{y}=\utilde{v}\cdotp\utidle{j}\)

Resolution of Velocity

We can convert a speed (\(v\)) and direction (\(\theta\)), to horizontal and vertical acceleration values:
\(\dot{x}=v\cos\theta\) and \(\dot{y}=v\sin\theta\)

We can use these to find some other helpful results:
\(v^2 = \dot{x}^2 + \dot{y}^2\) and \(\tan\theta = \frac{\dot{y}}{\dot{y}}\)

The Fundamentals of Projectile Motion

Projectile motion on Earth is affected by \(\utilde{a}=-g\utilde{j}\), where \(g\) is gravity, at about \(9.8m/s^2\).

Resulting from this, horizontal and vertical acceleration is as thus:
\(\ddot{x}=0\) and \(\ddot{y}=-g\).
